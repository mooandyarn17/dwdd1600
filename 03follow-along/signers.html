Who were the signers?

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Primary author of the Declaration. Later became the 3rd President of the United States. A key figure in American Enlightenment and political philosophy.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Fierce advocate for independence in the Continental Congress. Later became the 2nd President of the U.S. Also helped draft the Declaration.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned diplomat, scientist, and writer. Played a key role in gaining French support during the Revolutionary War. Oldest signer at age 70.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress at the time. Known for his bold and large signature â€” so much so that "John Hancock" became slang for a signature in the U.S.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Leader in organizing resistance against British rule (e.g., Boston Tea Party). Cousin of John Adams and a strong voice for independence.
Roger Sherman (Connecticut)
Only person to sign all four major U.S. founding documents.
Robert Morris (Pennsylvania)
Major financier of the Revolution.
Charles Carroll (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer; also the last surviving signer (died in 1832).

These individuals, among the 56 signers of the Declaration of Independence, played crucial roles in shaping the foundation of the United States. Their bold decision to publicly endorse the Declaration was an act of great personal risk, as it amounted to treason against the British Crown. By signing their names, they not only declared the colonies' intent to form a new nation but also laid the groundwork for the freedoms and democratic ideals that continue to define the United States today. Their legacy lives on as a testament to courage, vision, and the enduring fight for liberty.
