Crochet Lingo You Need to Know

Here you'll find clear explanations of the terms, abbreviations, and phrases commonly used in the world of crochet — perfect for beginners and a handy refresher for seasoned crafters.

Types of Crochet Lingo

Term Description Example
WIP (Work In Progress) A project currently being worked on. “Here's my WIP blanket for #MondayMotivation”
CAL (Crochet-Along) A community project where many people work on the same pattern or theme together. “Joining the #CAL2025 — excited to start!”
Stash Busting Using leftover or excess yarn from previous projects to make something new. “This baby blanket is a total stash buster!”
Frog/Frogging To undo your work (rip it out) when a mistake is made. “Major frog session happening tonight after my mistake…”
Yarn Chicken The suspense when you're finishing a project and unsure if you have enough yarn to complete it. “Playing yarn chicken on this last round…”
